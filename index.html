    Perceptual Confidence

    Currently, I have a range of perceptual confidence experiments underway. I am interested in how we form subjective estimates of performance in static and dynamic environments, as well as in environments with differing contexts.

    Audiovisual Integration

    How do we decide if a stream of sounds and light flashes originate from the same origin? How do the presence of these causal inference cues change behaviour? This project bridged results found in multisensory perception studies and the causal inference studies.

  • Locke, S. M., Landy, M. S. (2017) “Temporal Causal Inference With Stochastic Audiovisual Sequences.” PLoS ONE: 12(9), e0183776.

    • Auditory Motion Perception

    I am very passionate about learning how the brain perceives moving sounds. In one study I show that humans struggle to detect changes in velocity at particular speeds. In another I present evidence that the brain is using predictive mechanisms to track a moving sound.

  • Locke, S. M., Leung, J., and Carlile, S. (2016) “Sensitivity to Auditory Velocity Contrast.” Scientific Reports: 6, 27725.
  • Feinkohl, A., Locke, S. M., Leung, J., and Carlile, S. (2014) “The effect of velocity on auditory representational momentum.” JASA, 136, EL20.

    • Visual Attention

    By using an advanced projector display, we reasses a peculiar finding that invisible flickering stimuli can capture attention. We find no support for this phenomenon.

  • Alais, D., Locke, S. M., Leung, J., and Van der Burg, E. (2016) “No attentional capture from invisible flicker.” Scientific Reports: 6, 29296.
